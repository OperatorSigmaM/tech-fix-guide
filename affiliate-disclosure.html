What Are Affiliate Links?

Affiliate links are special tracking links that allow us to earn a commission when you purchase products through them. Here's how it works:

  1. You click an affiliate link on our website (e.g., a product recommendation)
  2. You're redirected to the merchant's website (Amazon, Newegg, etc.)
  3. You make a purchase (the price is the same - you don't pay extra)
  4. We earn a commission from the merchant (typically 1-10% of sale price)
üí° Important: Affiliate commissions do NOT increase the price you pay. Prices are identical whether you use our link or not. We simply receive a small percentage from the seller as a referral fee.

What Happens When You Click Affiliate Links

Cookie Tracking

When you click an affiliate link, a cookie is stored on your device. This cookie:

  • Tracks referrals: Tells the merchant you came from our website
  • Has limited duration: Usually 24 hours to 90 days depending on merchant
  • Attributes commissions: Ensures we receive credit for your purchase
  • Doesn't identify you personally: Anonymous tracking only

Your Privacy

Affiliate tracking is anonymous. Merchants know someone clicked from our site, but NOT:

  • Your name or personal information
  • What else you do on our website
  • Your browsing history outside the purchase

See our Privacy Policy for complete information on data collection.

When We DON'T Use Affiliate Links

Not all product mentions are affiliate links. We link to products without affiliate tracking when:

Our affiliate relationships constitute "material connections" under FTC guidelines, and we disclose them clearly to maintain transparency with our readers.
